Software Development Life Cycle

The process of guiding software development through phases such as planning, analysis, design, coding and more.

Agile Description

a software development approach emphasizing iterative cycles (like sprints), collaboration, and flexibility to respond to change.

Agile phases Description
Concept The initial stage where a project's vision, scope, and high-level goals are defined and prioritized.
Inception The initial phase for aligning stakeholders and the team on the project's vision, scope, and feasibility before starting development.
Iteration A short, fixed-length development cycle, typically one to four weeks long, where a team works to deliver a working piece of the product.
Release A release in Agile means delivering a new or improved part of the product to real users. It's the moment when ideas and work become something usable, something that creates value outside the team.
Maintenance The final operations phase of the software lifecycle, focusing on continuous support, bug fixes, and ongoing enhancements based on user feedback.
Retirement The final stage where a product is removed from production because it has reached the end of its life, often to be replaced by a new system.
